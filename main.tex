\begin{abstract}
Digital Mission Engineering (DME) ecosystems require multiple stakeholders to share engineering artifacts through a shared Authoritative Source of Truth (ASOT), yet current implementations lack cryptographic tamper evidence, immutable accountability trails, and mechanisms for verifying artifact provenance without exposing proprietary content.
We propose the \emph{assurance passport}, a W3C verifiable credential profile that combines consortium blockchain with zero-knowledge proofs to address these gaps.
An assurance passport attests to four properties of an engineering artifact---schema compliance, version validity, authorized origin, and derivation lineage---via Groth16 zk-SNARK proofs, without disclosing the artifact itself.
Commitments are anchored on a GoQuorum consortium blockchain, providing immutable temporal integrity and change accountability under a Byzantine fault-tolerant consensus model.
We present a vendor-neutral system architecture integrating ZK proof generation, on-chain anchoring, and a production DME platform (Kompozition) via a non-invasive adapter.
A proof-of-concept deployed on four NVIDIA Jetson Nano edge boards demonstrates feasibility under Denied, Degraded, Intermittent, and Limited (DDIL) connectivity conditions, with benchmarks across IBFT, QBFT, and Raft consensus protocols.
The assurance passport resolves the tension between transparency for trust and confidentiality for intellectual property protection, enabling privacy-preserving artifact sharing in coalition DME environments.
\end{abstract}
